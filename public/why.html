Why AgaPay

Mainstream giving platforms were engineered for generic, corporate-style ministries. AgaPay was built from within the Holy Tradition — by someone who knows the difference between a panakhida and a parastas.

What sets us apart

Six things no competitor can replicate

These aren't features that were added to serve Orthodox parishes. They are the foundation AgaPay was built on from day one.

Built from within the Tradition

AgaPay was built by a tonsured Reader of ROCOR — not a fintech company that decided to enter the religious market. Every feature was shaped by someone who lives this life from the inside.

The liturgical calendar is native

Both Julian and Gregorian calendars are supported. Campaigns are tied to the festal cycle — Nativity, Theophany, Great Lent, Pascha, Soul Saturdays — not arbitrary marketing windows.

Commemoration lists go to the priest

When a parishioner submits a candle offering or memorial donation, the departed names are formatted and routed directly to the priest's proskomedia queue. Nothing is lost.

Canonical verification protects the faithful

Every parish and monastery is reviewed for canonical standing before activation. When an Orthodox Christian gives through AgaPay, they know it reaches a legitimate community.

Free for monasteries — permanently

Monasteries and sketes pay no monthly platform fee, ever. This decision was made at founding and will not change. It is not a promotion. It is a conviction.

Month-to-month — no lock-ins

No annual contracts. No cancellation penalties. No two-year commitments. A platform should earn its parishes every month — not imprison them.

"The Church does not give because she is asked. She gives because she loves. AgaPay is built around that truth."

— AgaPay founding principle
