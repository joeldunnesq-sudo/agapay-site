The story

Nothing I write on paper will live beyond me.

I have spent over a decade in courtrooms and at desks, building cases, pursuing justice, and serving the public. It is honorable, demanding work. But over the years, I came to understand a quiet reality: nothing I do in the legal system will truly live beyond me in the memories of those I love. Verdicts are rendered, files are archived, and the paper fades.

That is not a counsel of despair; it is a call to build something in a different register. I wanted to build something that serves the Body of Christ directly. Something that shows my ten children what it means to put one's hands to work for the Church, helping to secure the growth and maturation of Holy Orthodoxy in the American land.

That is why AgaPay exists.

"I want to create something worthwhile — something that makes a tangible difference in people's lives."

The need

The Church deserves a fully integrated giving platform.

When I looked at the giving platforms adopted by Orthodox parishes, I saw tools built for a completely different theology. They were engineered for generic, mass-market ministries. They didn't know what a panakhida was. They had no concept of the Typikon. They were blind to the liturgical rhythm of our life: the feast days, Soul Saturdays, and Great Lenten appeals that shape how Orthodox Christians naturally give.

I am a tonsured Reader. I have stood in the nave, held a taper at Pascha, and submitted names for the proskomedia. I know this life from the inside. You cannot replicate that understanding by hiring a tech consultant or bolting on a 'custom feature' to a megachurch platform.

AgaPay was built from within the Tradition. Every line of code, every workflow, and every design decision was made by someone who lives the Orthodox life.

The vision

A platform that outlasts its founder.

Holy Orthodoxy is still young in America. The parishes being built today, the monasteries being established, and the children being raised in the faith are laying a foundation that will endure for centuries, if we tend it faithfully.

AgaPay is a piece of that tending. It is a digital infrastructure designed to make parishes financially resilient and to ensure monasteries receive the vital, unburdened support they need. It allows the faithful to give with the exact same intentionality and reverence they bring to every other aspect of their liturgical life.

I want my children to see that their father built something in service of the Church, something real, something useful, and something that carries the faith forward. To me, that is worth more than any verdict or title."
