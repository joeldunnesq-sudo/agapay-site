Three Ways the Church Gives

✝

Most giving platforms treat every donation the same. AGAPAY understands that a weekly tithe, a memorial offering, and a relief campaign are fundamentally different acts — and treats them accordingly.

Stewardship illustration

Regular Stewardship

Faithful. Recurring. Foundational.
  • Weekly and monthly recurring pledges
  • Annual stewardship campaigns
  • Liturgical calendar timing — Great Lent, Pascha, Nativity
  • Giving statements for tax purposes
  • Parishioner giving history and pledge tracking

Designated Funds

Purposeful. Transparent. Permanent.
  • Building and renovation funds
  • Iconostasis and beautification campaigns
  • Seminary and education scholarships
  • Parish school endowments
  • Restricted-fund tracking with transparent reporting

Campaign Giving

Urgent. Time-bound. Verified.
  • Disaster and emergency relief appeals
  • Health crisis and medical funds
  • Love offerings for clergy and families in need
  • Time-limited feast day campaigns
  • Canonically verified — every campaign vetted

From registration to first offering.

✝

AGAPAY is designed to be set up by a parish council member, not a software engineer. If you can send an email, you can set up AGAPAY.

1

Parish registers with AGAPAY

A priest or parish council administrator submits a registration request. We review canonical standing before activation, so only recognized Orthodox communities use the platform.

2

Configure your parish profile

Set your jurisdiction, liturgical calendar (Julian or Gregorian), patronal feast, accepted offering types, and language preferences.

3

Share with your parishioners

Every parish gets a clean, dedicated giving page. Share it in your bulletin, from the ambo, or by email. No app download required for donors.

4

Names reach the priest

When a parishioner submits a candle offering or memorial donation, the names are formatted and routed toward the priest's commemoration preparation.

5

The treasurer sees everything clearly

Every offering is categorized, dated, and attributed, with a complete dashboard for the parish treasurer.
