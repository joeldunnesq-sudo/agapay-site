The AGAPAY Learn planner gives your household five views. Plan at the horizon that fits the moment: a quick look at today, the full week board, a month calendar with feast and fast days marked, a term pacing grid, and a year-level overview of your cycle and season topics.

  • Week board with household stream rows and per-child lesson columns

Grace Mode

Your plan should serve your family, not punish it.

When the normal full plan no longer fits the day, Grace Mode lets you lighten it without deleting work or pretending the plan never existed. Choose one of four Grace modes, and AGAPAY automatically adjusts which subjects are kept, reduced, or deferred — while preserving prayer, read-aloud, and core rhythms.

  • Four Grace modes: Light, Minimum Viable, Feast Only, and Custom
  • Each subject can be marked Keep, Reduce First, or Defer If Needed
  • Deferred work moves to the reserve list — not deleted
  • Works for illness, travel, new babies, feast days, or any hard season
  • Change Grace Mode from day to day as family life changes
  • Mode is visible in the planner week board as a ribbon on each row
Light Keeps essentials and softens lower-priority work. Most common.
Minimum Viable Keeps prayer, one shared touchpoint, and the next right thing.
Feast Only Church rhythm and feast celebration only — everything academic steps aside.
Custom You decide exactly what stays, reduces, and defers.

How planning works

Two layers. One household. Total flexibility.

AGAPAY Learn uses a two-layer model. Shared enrichment — Church rhythms, read-alouds, catechesis, hymns — is planned once for the household. Core subjects are assigned by Form, grade, or individual child.

⌂

Family-based

Shared learning for everyone together: Church rhythms, morning basket, read-alouds, catechesis, hymns, saints, poetry, artists, and nature walks.

◎

Form-based or grade-based

Group children at a similar stage into Forms and plan their core subjects once — or keep traditional grade and level assignments if you prefer.

▣

Individual child tracks

Assign specific books, subjects, or tasks to a single child where needed. Child-level lesson sheets print from the Print Center.

☩

Church calendar backbone

Both Julian and Revised Julian calendars are supported. Feast days, fasting rules, and season markers shape every view in the planner.

Getting started

A guided setup wizard. A real first week.

The AGAPAY Learn Setup Wizard walks you through your household in five steps. When you finish, Learn can create a real, editable starter week — not a demo.

1

Household basics

Name your household, choose your Church calendar (Julian or Revised Julian), and set your school year dates.

2

Method & groups

Choose Charlotte Mason, Orthodox Classical, Traditional, Eclectic, or Unsure. Pick Forms or grade levels.

3

Add your children

First name, age, grade or Form. The free plan includes up to two children; Family plans are unlimited.

4

Grace Mode

Learn how Grace Mode works and choose your household default. You can change it any day.

5

Starter week

Optionally create a real editable first term — Morning Prayers, Daily Readings, Saint of the Day, read-aloud, nature walk, and starter subjects for every Form or grade.

⚙

Advanced Setup

Add subjects, books, formation materials, enrichment blocks, and terms. Connect Google Calendar when ready. Set Grace Mode priorities per subject.

Optional Google Calendar Sync

Your plan can travel with your family calendar.

When a signed-in parent chooses to connect Google Calendar, AGAPAY Learn requests only the Calendar events permission. It creates and updates selected lesson blocks, term dates, feast days, and reminders in the user's primary calendar, and checks previously synced AGAPAY Learn events to prevent duplicates. Calendar connection is optional; AGAPAY Learn works without it. AGAPAY Learn does not sell Google user data or use Google Calendar data for advertising. Users may disconnect or revoke access at any time.
